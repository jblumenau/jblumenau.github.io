He is a Labour MP who comes from a white working class background. He is from a constituency that is not your constituency. He rarely speaks out and votes against his party leadership. He supports the construction of thousands of new onshore wind turbines. In political debates, he regularly explains how his decisions help to promote the national interest. Asked recently about the role and duties of politicians, he said:  “Elections are important because they force me as a politician to listen and respond to the views of voters.”
